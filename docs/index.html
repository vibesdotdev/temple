Current Status

Phase: Post-hoc control mapping is active. Large scratch training is paused until control effects are replicated across families with matched baselines.

This public report now prioritizes high-confidence claims tied to reproducible artifacts. Older narrative sections that were not fully synchronized with run history have been trimmed.

What We Know Now

  • Displacement calibration is mandatory for cross-family transfer. Fixed absolute perturbation scales do not transfer reliably.
  • In the Devstral family, calibrated crystal variants consistently outperform displacement-matched Gaussian controls on strict-format robustness in repeated 500-task runs.
  • A stricter semantic scorer for one-word tasks removed a known permissiveness artifact and reduced inflated semantic scores in Gaussian controls.
  • A structured orthogonal control at matched displacement did not preserve behavior as well as calibrated crystal in the latest 4-way run.
  • In Qwen3-4B, strict behavior direction is inverted relative to Devstral: matched controls score above base on strict format across three seeds.

What Is Still Open

  • Whether the observed effects generalize across more model families and sizes.
  • Whether geometric method effects remain superior after full control matching and seed replication in every lane.
  • Whether the effect is mostly a format/style control, or can be made to steer deeper reasoning behavior with low capability cost.
